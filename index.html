Commissioned Officer Career Timeline

From submitting an application to taking the oath of commission as a Lieutenant, Sub Lieutenant, or Flying Officer.

Phase 1

Notification & Application Selection

Keep track of UPSC gazettes or direct recruitment entries (SSC Tech, TGC, JAG, NCC). Eligible candidates apply online. Non-written entries are shortlisted purely based on graduation/engineering cut-off scores.

Phase 2

5-Day Service Selection Board (SSB)

Candidates report to Selection Centres (e.g., Allahabad, Bhopal, Bangalore, Kapurthala). Over five days, they face psychological screening (PP&DT), Group GTO tasks, individual obstacles, personal interviews, and the board conference. This screens for Officer Like Qualities (OLQs).

Phase 3

Specialist Medical Boards

Recommended candidates undergo comprehensive medical examination at Military Hospitals (SMB/AMB). Checked for eye vision limits, flat foot, knock knees, DNS, cardiovascular health, and surgical standards. Temporary issues get 42 days for appeal.

Phase 4

All India Merit List & Academy Training

UPSC and Recruiting Directorates prepare the final merit list based on written scores and SSB marks. Selected candidates report to their respective Service Training Academies (IMA Dehradun, OTA Chennai, INA Ezhimala, AFA Dundigal) for rigorous military training (49 to 74 weeks).

Phase 5

Passing Out Parade & Commission

The culmination of academy training. On passing out, gentlemen and lady cadets take the oath of allegiance to the Constitution, receive their ranks (Stars on Shoulders), and get commissioned as Group A Gazetted Officers in their respective forces.
